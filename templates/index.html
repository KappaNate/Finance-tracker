User Guide

About This Guide

This guide explains the features of the Finance Tracking App and how the core logic works. To reopen it at any time, click the ? button in the top-right corner of the app.

Example Database

The app opens with a pre-loaded database called Example, designed to give you a realistic starting point for exploring all of the app's features before setting up your own finances.

The Example database contains three accounts across three months (April – June 2026):

  • Checking Account — a fully categorised everyday spending account with income, bills, groceries, dining, transportation, personal expenses, an annual category (Car Registration), and a Reserve category (Emergency Fund). Categories are organised into folders.
  • Savings Account — two savings categories (Emergency Fund and Vacation Fund) receiving monthly transfers from Checking.
  • Visa Credit Card — a Debt account with a starting balance, recurring charges, and monthly payments transferred from Checking. Pay-by date and minimum due are set.

June is partially filled to show a realistic mid-month view, including pending transactions and upcoming pay-by dates visible in the Payment Calendar.

When you are ready to start tracking your own finances, open Manage Databases from the database picker in the top-left and create a new database. You can keep the Example database around as a reference or delete it at any time.

Overview

This app is a month-by-month personal finance tracker. Each month you navigate to is its own snapshot: you can see what you earned, what you budgeted, and what you actually spent. All data is stored locally in SQLite databases on your machine.

Navigating Months

Use the Year and Month dropdowns in the header to move between periods. Click + Add Month inside the Month dropdown to create a new tracked month. Each month is independent, transactions and per-month category values (budget amount, pay-by date, and minimum due) can differ from month to month. Account and category notes, however, are persistent and stay the same regardless of which month you are viewing.

Summary Cards

The three cards at the top of the page show totals for the viewed month across all accounts:

  • Total Income — sum of all income transactions.
  • Total Expenses — sum of all expense transactions.
  • Balance — income minus expenses for the month.

Accounts

Accounts are the top-level containers for your finances. Click an account header to expand or collapse it. Drag accounts to reorder them. Each account has a type that controls how it is summarised:

  • Checking / Savings / Cash — standard accounts. Shows income, budgeted amount, amount spent, and remaining balance.
  • Debt (credit card, loan, etc.) — tracks a starting balance, charges & interest added during the month, payments made, and the remaining balance owed.
  • Investment — tracks deposits, withdrawals, gains/losses, and net balance.

Hover over the ⚙ icon on an account to access options for editing its name, type, or date range, adding notes, or deleting it. The Start / End Month fields let you hide an account outside its active date range.

Categories & Folders

Inside each account, categories represent individual budget line items (e.g., Rent, Groceries, Salary). Folders let you group related categories together — drag a category onto a folder to nest it, or drag it out to un-nest it.

Each category has a budgeted amount and a live spending progress bar that fills as transactions are added. Category types affect how they behave:

  • Budget — standard expense category for tracking spending against a set limit.
  • Income — money coming in; counts toward the month's Total Income.
  • Transfer — moves money between two accounts without counting as income or expense.
  • Loan — tracks payments toward a loan balance in a Debt account.
  • Credit Card — tracks payments toward a credit card balance in a Debt account.
  • Investment — records contributions or withdrawals in an Investment account.
  • Interest — auto-calculated interest charges added to a Debt account.

Hover over the ⚙ icon on a category to access options for editing its budget, type, pay-by date, minimum due, date range, and notes, or to delete it.

Transactions

Transactions are individual income or expense records attached to a category. Click + Add inside a category row to open the transaction form. Fields include:

  • Date — defaults to the first of the viewed month. A warning appears if you enter a date outside the viewed month.
  • Description & Payee — free-text labels for the transaction.
  • Payment Method — optional tag (e.g., Credit Card, Cash).
  • Amount — the transaction value.

Hover over the ⚙ icon next to any transaction to edit or delete it, or to add a note.

Check Mark as pending when adding or editing a transaction to flag it as not yet settled. Pending transactions display with a dimmed style and a Pending badge, and are excluded from all balance and total calculations until the checkbox is cleared.

Transfers

Transfers move money between two accounts (e.g., paying a credit card from checking). They appear in both the source and destination accounts as income and expense, but do not count as income or expense in the summary cards. Add a transfer via the Transfer category type or through the dedicated transfer form on a Debt account.

Credit Card & Loan Tracking

Credit card and loan balances live inside a Debt account using a Credit Card or Loan category. The two types are functionally identical — the distinction is purely for labeling. Each category has a starting balance representing what was owed when you began tracking it.

The Debt account summary is built from four values:

  • Starting Debt — the balance carried in from prior months: the initial starting balance plus all previous charges, minus all previous payments. This is calculated automatically each month.
  • Charges / Interest — expense transactions added in the current month.
  • Payments — income transactions applied in the current month.
  • Remaining Balance — Starting Debt + Charges − Payments.

Recording a charge: Add an expense transaction to the Credit Card or Loan category. This increases the debt balance for the month.

Recording a payment: Use a Transfer from your checking account to the Debt account. This does two things at once — it records an expense in the checking account (reducing its balance) and an income entry in the Debt account (reducing the amount owed). The transfer out of checking is excluded from the overall summary cards so the payment is counted only once, not as both an expense and an income.

Because each month's starting debt is derived from the full history of prior transactions, the balance carries forward correctly on its own — no manual adjustment is needed when moving between months.

Interest Tracking

Debt accounts can have automatic interest calculated each month. Set an interest rate (percentage) or a flat monthly amount on the account. The app will generate an Interest transaction automatically, keeping your debt balance accurate without manual entry.

Savings & Reserved

Within a Savings (or Checking/Cash) account, you can create Reserve categories to carve out dedicated pools of money — for example, an emergency fund or a vacation savings bucket. Each Reserve category has its own starting balance and tracks deposits and withdrawals independently from the rest of the account.

The account summary reflects reserved funds in two ways:

  • Reserved — the combined balance of all Reserve categories in the account.
  • Unreserved — the account's net balance minus the total reserved amount, showing what is freely available.

Separately, budget categories can be marked as Annual. These represent expenses that recur yearly (e.g., insurance, subscriptions). When any annual categories are active, the account summary shows an Annual Reserved total — the sum of their budgeted amounts — as a reminder of long-term commitments built into that month's budget. This value is informational and does not reduce any balance.

Payment Calendar

The calendar icon button below the month/year selectors opens the Payment Calendar — a full month grid that gives an at-a-glance view of upcoming obligations and unresolved transactions. Two types of events are shown:

  • Pay-by dates — due dates set on Credit Card or Loan categories, indicating when a payment is expected.
  • Pending transactions — any transaction marked as pending, showing the date it is assigned to.

Hover over any day on the calendar to see a tooltip with the payee, amount, and description for each event on that date. Items that have no date assigned appear in a table below the calendar grid.

Charts

A bar chart at the bottom of the page visualises spending by category for the selected account. Use the account selector above the chart to switch which account is displayed.

Multiple Databases

The database picker in the top-left of the header lets you switch between separate SQLite database files. This is useful for keeping personal and business finances separate, tracking finances in different currencies, or for maintaining an archive. Click Manage Databases to create, rename, or delete database files.

Database Encryption

Each database can be protected with a password using AES-256 encryption (SQLCipher). To manage a database's password, open Manage Databases from the database picker, then click the Security button next to the database you want to configure.

  • Add password — encrypts the database file. From this point on, the password is required every time the app starts.
  • Change password — re-encrypts the database with a new password. The current password is required to make this change.
  • Remove password — decrypts the database back to a standard, unencrypted SQLite file. The current password is required.

When the app starts with an encrypted database, an Unlock prompt appears before any data is loaded. If you enter the wrong password, nothing is written or changed — the database remains locked.

Your password is only held in memory for the duration of the session and is never written to disk. Closing the app clears it, so you will be prompted again on the next launch.

Important: there is no password recovery mechanism. If you forget your password, the database cannot be opened. Keep a backup of the unencrypted database or store the password somewhere safe before enabling encryption.

Settings & Reports

Open Settings from the gear menu to change the currency symbol displayed throughout the app. Changing the displayed currency does not convert the values.

Settings also includes an Accent Color picker and a Border Color picker for personalising the app's appearance. Accent Color controls the highlight color used for active elements and labels throughout the interface; Border Color controls the shade of dividers and panel borders. Both have a Reset button to return to the default colors. These preferences are saved locally within the app and are device-specific — they do not carry over when switching devices or reinstalling the app.

Open Report Generator from the gear menu to export data to CSV or PDF — choose which months, accounts, and categories to include, optionally include individual transactions, then click Download.
